Hypothesis Testing¶

We use hypothesis testing to determine whether or not a hypothesis about a population is true. This is important in data science because it allows us to draw conclusions from our data and make informed decisions based on statistical evidence. For our data, we are going to do a one sample t-test to see if the average temperatures from 1980 onwards are significantly higher than the overall average temperature. In this case, the null hypothesis would be that there is no change in average temperature, while the alternative hypothesis is that there is an increase in average temperature. To get test statistic, we will use the SciPy stats module:

In [11]:
import scipy.stats as stats

t_statistic, p_value = stats.ttest_1samp(a=data2['Temperature'].iloc[85:], popmean=data2['Temperature'].mean())
print("t-statistic:", t_statistic)
print("p-value:", p_value)

t-statistic: 5.372312120260752
p-value: 3.591811008296598e-06

With a p-value of 0.00000359 its safe to say we can reject the null hypothesis.

Conclusion¶

It's clear that there is strong evidence that climate change is real. The data we analyzed supports the hypothesis that the average temperature in the contiguous United States is increasing over time. These findings are in line with the overwhelming scientific consensus on the existence of climate change and further highlight the need to address this issue.
